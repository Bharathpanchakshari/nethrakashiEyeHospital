F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment by calling our hospital's main phone number, using our online appointment booking system, or visiting the hospital in person. Our staff will assist you in finding a convenient time for your visit.

2. What are the common signs that I need to see an eye doctor?

Symptoms include blurred vision, eye pain, redness, excessive tearing, double vision, flashes of light, floaters, headaches related to vision, or difficulty seeing at night.

3. What services does the hospital provide?

Services may include vision testing, cataract treatment, glaucoma management, LASIK and refractive surgery, retinal care, pediatric ophthalmology, contact lens fitting, and emergency eye care.

4. How long does a comprehensive eye examination take?

A routine eye exam typically takes 30–60 minutes, though specialized tests may require additional time.

5. When should I get my eyes examined?

Adults should generally have a comprehensive eye exam every 1–2 years, depending on age, vision status, and risk factors. Children and people with diabetes or other eye conditions may need more frequent exams.

6. Can children receive eye care services?

Yes, children can receive eye care services. Pediatric ophthalmologists specialize in diagnosing and treating eye conditions in children, including vision screenings, eye exams, and treatment for conditions such as amblyopia (lazy eye) and strabismus (crossed eyes).
